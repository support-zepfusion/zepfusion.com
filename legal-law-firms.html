Trust & privacy

Confidentiality by design

Clients expect discretion; regulators and insurers expect evidence of control. Your systems should make who saw what, and when obvious—not something you reconstruct from memory.

For assisted drafting and triage, the same discipline applies: suggestions versus approvals logged, bounded by your policy—not a black box. For artefacts that must stand up to scrutiny, we can add a clear record of version and timing alongside human custody—they support integrity; they do not replace privilege rules or local procedure.

If you later need stronger governance between offices or counsel—separate brands, referral networks, or enterprise policies—we can grow with you step by step.

Your firm, your boundaries

Matters stay scoped to the right teams; access follows role—partner, associate, paralegal, finance—so people see only what they need. Encryption, retention, and who can record or seal critical artefacts stay under your rules. Branding and client experience stay yours, not a generic vendor shell.

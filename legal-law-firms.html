Trust & privacy

Confidentiality by design

Clients expect discretion; regulators and insurers expect evidence of control. Your systems should make who saw what, and when obvious—not something you reconstruct from memory.

For AI-assisted steps, the same discipline applies: suggestions versus approvals logged, models and prompts bounded by your policy—not a black box. For artefacts that must stand up to scrutiny, content IDs and anchored hashes complement human custody; they support integrity and timeline—they do not replace privilege rules or local procedure.

If you later need stronger governance between offices or counsel—separate brands, referral networks, or enterprise policies—we can grow with you step by step.

Your firm, your boundaries

Matters stay scoped to the right teams; access follows role—partner, associate, paralegal, finance—so people see only what they need. Encryption, retention, and who pins or anchors what stay under your rules. Branding and client experience stay yours, not a generic vendor shell.
